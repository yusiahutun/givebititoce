    Chemmy Alcott: Ill get ice dance duo knighted!

    And the Olympic skier reckons she can make it happen thanks to her friendship with the Queens granddaughter Zara Phillips, 30. Chemmy, 29, who is currently battling it out on Dancing On Ices nationwide tour, believes its about time Jayne Torvill, 54, and Christopher Dean, 53, were handed more medals.

    02 May 2024, 00:00

    And the Olympic skier reckons she can make it happen thanks to her friendship with the Queen’s granddaughter Zara Phillips, 30.

    Chemmy, 29, who is currently battling it out on Dancing On Ice’s nationwide tour, believes it’s about time Jayne Torvill, 54, and Christopher Dean, 53, were handed more medals.

    She told us: “It seems bizarre to me that they haven’t been knighted because they ­totally should be. I want to get them knighted. I know Zara so maybe if I say to her: ‘Hey can you call up your grandma and get Torvill and Dean knighted?’

    “I’m not sure if the royals are fans but I think everyone loves a bit of glitz, glamour and competition.

    “Next time I meet the Queen I will ask her whether she watched it.”

    Meanwhile Chemmy says she never expected the reaction she’s had from her involvement in the ITV1 skating show.

    She added: “What I didn’t realise was me on Dancing On Ice would inspire people who have had injuries to get back and start being ­active again. It feels people are listening to me more.

    “I am one of the Olympic torch bearers which is ­fantastic and I’m really excited to be a part of my home games.

    “I feel that Dancing On Ice has given me the platform to be so much more influential to the things that I support like charities.

    “I would love to set up my own charity one day.”
