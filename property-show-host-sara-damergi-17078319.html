    In the hit daytime show Sara, 36, and co-host Kerr Drummond showcase the most beautiful coastal and rural locations in the UK, with the aim of helping buyers decide where to buy their dream home.

    Sara, who also hosts Channel 4's A Place In The Sun, took a break from the house-hunting to tell us about her globe-trotting lifestyle.

    You've said you think anyone can escape the rat race, I'm sure a lot of people would like to know how?

    I have an online business which allowed me to travel the world as a digital nomad. I believe the Internet is going to be responsible for power being handed back to the people rather than the bigger corporations. We can all earn money online and create our own freedom. I think as long as it's an honest passion or it helps people you can make a business from it.

    You've done work on helping young people break into TV is that something you're passionate about?

    Yes I have a subscription based website called www.castingnow.co.uk I set it up for two reasons because it's tough to break into the TV and film industry without an agent and secondly to allow myself freedom to work from anywhere. It's really lovely to see the testimonials from people who have had success.

    You're 'country' on the show but do you sometimes prefer the coastal properties?

    Never! My competitor Kerr on the show would never let me hear the last of it if I said yes. But my dad lives on the coast of the Mediterranean Sea so I see the benefit when it's warm but the climate in the UK means the sea just isn't for me!

    You've presented a place in the sun too so have seen a fair share of properties, where has been your absolute favourite?

    My absolute favourite location was Puerta Vieja in Costa Rica. Showering in tropical heat in the rainforest in bathrooms with no roofs is an experience that's pretty tough to beat.

    If you have a dilemma between buying on the coast or in the country, are planning to buy in 2017 and would like to appear on the programme, please email: househunters@coastvcountry.co.uk

    Coast vs Country is on weekdays at 3pm on Channel 4
