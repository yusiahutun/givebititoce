    1

    Hermès 24 Faubourg

    .

    Perfume is one of the most personal beauty choices that someone can make, so it's no wonder that we're so intrigued by the scents that celebrities wear. Hermès 24 Faubourg was revealed to be one of Diana's favourite fragrances by her butler Paul Burrell, containing delicate notes of hyacinth and citrus for a classic and light-hearted scent.

    Hermès 24 Faubourg, £130 SHOP NOW

    2

    ByTerry Baume De Rose

    .

    The cult multipurpose product is adored by beauty obsessives the world over, so it's no surprise that legendary make-up artist Mary Greenwell counts it as a staple. Sharing the beauty tips and products she used on Diana via Lisa Eldridge's YouTube channel, Greenwell sang the praises of ByTerry's Baume de Rose Lip Care for providing a moisturising base.

    ByTerry Baume de Rose, £40 SHOP NOW

    3

    Max Factor Foundation

    .

    Make-up artist Clayton Howard was responsible for creating the soft-focus look for Princess Diana's Vogue shoot that went on to provide her engagement photo. In the face chart that has since resurfaced, Howard chronicled his use of Max Factor for the majority of her base make-up, specifically the brand's Sheer Genius Foundation. While this has since been discontinued, the Miracle Second Skin Hybrid Foundation gives an almost identical glowing finish to the skin.

    Max Factor Miracle Touch Second Skin Foundation, £12.99 SHOP NOW

    4

    Clarins Multi Active Day Cream

    .

    Said to suffer with rosacea, Princess Diana took special care to look after her skin and was selective with the brands she chose to use. Clarins was reportedly one of the princess' staple skincare brands (also a favourite of the Queen), with the brand's Multi-Active Day Cream and Cleansing Milk with Alpine Herbs (now discontinued) was often used in her daily routine.

    Clarins Multi-Active Day Cream SPF20, £43 SHOP NOW

    5

    Blue Kohl Liner

    .

    Another product credited in Howard's face chart was Elizabeth Arden's Blue Kohl in 636. While this particular product has since been discontinued, the vibrant cobalt blue was a shade that the younger Princess Diana often opted for. For a modern equivalent, try Dior's Waterproof Crayon Eyeliner in Captivating Blue.

    Dior Waterproof Crayon Eyeliner in Captivating Blue, £22 SHOP NOW

    6

    Rosewater Toner

    .

    Another skincare product Princess Diana reportedly couldn't be without was rosewater, a product she religiously applied to her skin to keep it soothed and hydrated. While the exact rosewater she used is unknown, it's likely that due to her rosacea that she preferred uncomplicated formulas made with sensitive skin types in mind.

    Fresh Rose Deep Hydration Facial Toner, £35 SHOP NOW

    Penhaligons Bluebell Eau De Toilette

    .

    As quintessentially English as Princess Diana herself, another one of her most favoured perfumes was Bluebell by Penhaligon's. With accords of green and floral, this perfume evokes a walk through the woods in spring and was a nostalgic scent which Diana wore in her younger years.
