    Seven signs why your neighbors think you're a swinger

    Your innocent attempts to spruce up your house and front yard may be sending an unwanted signal to your neighbors that you're a swinger. Over the years rumors have emerged of how you can tell if a person indulges in the lifestyle.

    14 Mar 2024, 00:00

    Your innocent attempts to spruce up your house and front yard may be sending an unwanted signal to your neighbors that you're a swinger.

    Over the years rumors have emerged of how you can tell if a person indulges in the lifestyle.

    Most recently, a Florida retirement village made headlines when a TikTok video that claimed to show a color-coded loofah system used by senior swingers to indicate their sexual preferences went viral. 

    The video, which was posted by drag queen Tora Himan and has been viewed more than three million times, cycles through a series of photos of cars in a parking lot that have different colored loofahs hanging off them. 

    The TikTok creator tagged the location as Florida's The Villages, the world's largest retirement community that surpasses the size of Manhattan and encompasses five zip codes, with more than 130,000 residents. 

    With that in mind, FEMAIL has rounded up some of the tactics allegedly used by swingers to bag their next conquest - from the upside-down pineapple to garden gnomes.

     

    Pampas grass

    Its rumored association with swingers became so intense that it seemed to have led to a downturn in sales of the grass (stock image)

    Its rumored association with swingers became so intense that it seemed to have led to a downturn in sales of the grass, which had been a staple in front yards for decades.

    Nurseries in the UK reported demand for the variety had dropped off, with one store telling The Telegraph in 2017 they had been selling about 550 plants annually 10 years ago.

    Five years later that number had dipped below 500 and it was about 250 in 2017.

    In the US, Southern Living labeled the grass 'tacky' in a 2016 article headlined, Pampas Grass Is For Swingers, and questioned why it was so popular.

    It concluded its investigation by citing an annotation on an image of the grass, which the article's author said might explain 'what the pampas grass is really doing there.'

    '"It is a well established fact," writes Firky, "that if you have pampas grass in your garden you're going to attract swingers (swingers use the grass as an advert to other swingers). Has anyone got pampas grass in their garden and... had anyone knocking on their door asking to swing?"' the story quoted.

     

    Upside-down pineapples

    Unsuspecting cruise ship travelers have been spotting the upside-down pineapples on cabin doors

    Unsuspecting cruise ship travelers have been spotting the upside-down pineapples on cabin doors.

    And they're shocked to learn that its a secret sign used by swingers to identify each other in public.

    But the fruit also means the same thing on land, according to popular travel blog Cruise Mummy.

    If you spot an upside-down pineapple on a front porch, it could be an invitation to take part in a swingers party.

    A door knocker fashioned into the fruit could also be a sign your neighbor wants to partner-swap, The Sun reported.

     

    Garden gnomes

    A California-based real estate agent once shared a post about swingers apparently using garden gnomes to show their partner-swapping fetish

    A California-based real estate agent once shared a post about swingers apparently using garden gnomes to indicate their partner-swapping fetish.

    The agent said a client had been looking to buy in a North County San Diego neighborhood, which held 'family-friendly events throughout the year.'

    But it turns out that it might not be as wholesome as first thought.

    'My client told his friend he's looking to buy in this community. His friend informed him it's a huge swinger community,' the agent wrote.

    'How can you tell if your neighbors are swingers you may ask?

    'Turns out that homeowners keep gnomes on the front lawn as an indicator. My client looked online to see if this was a joke or real and sure enough, there's a website out there with info all about it.'

     

    Flamingos on the lawn

    Another garden ornament apparently on the swingers' radar are lawn flamingos

    Another garden ornament apparently on the swingers' radar are lawn flamingos.

    A quick Google search brings up many subreddits discussing the pink bird being a sign that you're part of the community.

    'I recently saw a thread on a Swedish kink site we're on stating that with the recent trend of flamingos on everything from lawn d√©cor to candles and shoes, the swinger world is giggling because the flamingo is a secret signal to other swingers,' the post read. 

    'News articles are also saying that this is a well known signal around the world.'

    Some people confirmed they had heard that the lawn decoration was used by swingers, but none were able to produce solid evidence it was actually a thing.

     

    White landscaping rocks

    Another item that's found its way into the swingers' lore is white landscaping rocks in your garden

    Another item that's found its way into the swingers' lore is white landscaping rocks in your garden.

    Cooper S. Beckett, who hosts the Life On The Swingset: The Swinging & Polyamory Podcast, wrote though most of the community were unsure about the myth's origins, some 'have placed white rocks in their front yards to reaffirm their "secret code swinger status."'

    A post from several years ago on the Swingers subreddit also mentioned this tactic, specifying they had heard it was white rocks around a mailbox.

    Meanwhile Urban Dictionary defines 'white rocked' as 'being propositioned by a swinger couple.'

    'The etymology of the phrase white rock comes from the urban legend that a house with decorative white rocks in the front is a signal that it is occupied by active swingers, and an open invitation,' it said.

     

    Adirondack chairs

    The different colors of the Adirondack chairs apparently have a hidden meaning

    The humble furniture is apparently a sign of swingers, with different colors having a hidden meaning, according to Urban Dictionary.

    A Redditor also posted about hearing the rumor from a relative in the New Jersey subreddit.

    'My sister-in-law told me that in Union County if you have 2 Adirondack chairs on your lawn, facing the road, it's how you announce that you're a swinger,' the social media user said.

    'Has anyone else heard this or know it to be true? I'm curious if this is true or just a dumb rumor.'

    Again a quick Google search brings up a handful of results linking the furniture to the lifestyle, but only conjecture thus far.

     

    Black rings on the right hand

    A black ring on the right hand could indicate someone is a swinger. Pictured is a stock image

    While perusing the internet about swingers, another commonly mentioned signal used by partner swappers was wearing a black ring.

    Cooper told The Stranger in 2015 you can wear the jewelry on any finger on your right hand, but not the middle as it was sometimes used as a 'symbol of asexuality.'

    'The black ring is a concept that's been gaining steam over the last couple years,' he said.

    Cooper also said: 'Wear your black ring on your right hand if you are out and open to meet other swingers. If someone wearing a black ring on their right hand approaches you, start a conversation with them, "I see you're wearing a black ring on your right hand. Maybe we are in the same club."'
