    Are you looking for a home in Kenya? If you are, it's advisable to learn about the housing market. Kenya's housing market is fair compared to other jurisdictions in the world. However, the market favours foreign investors who can afford real estate. However, only a few locals can afford these real estates, leaving them looking for other means of acquiring property. As a result, the government initiated an affordable housing project in Nairobi in 2017. The project aims to curb the housing menace for low-and middle-income citizens. But what is the progress so far? is the project finished? And what exactly is affordable housing in Nairobi?

    Nairobi's affordable housing programme is part of the Kenyan government's Big Four agenda. The agenda aims to promote long-term economic development by providing affordable accommodation, universal health coverage, improving manufacturing, and improving food security and nutrition. These projects will encourage ordinary citizens to contribute to the nation's growth and prosperity.

    The housing programme is the most visible of the ongoing projects. Some citizens are interested in the scheme's progress. So, how far along are the government's affordable housing projects?

    Affordable housing in Nairobi

    If you are a resident of Nairobi, you might have noticed the ongoing construction in various parts of the city. The structures are part of the Pangani affordable housing project under the Boma Yangu scheme. Completing these residential areas is almost complete, with most units selling out.

    But if you are new to this scheme, you might wonder what it is all about. Here are all the projects of the Boma Yangu houses in Nairobi.

    1. Pangani affordable housing project

    The Pangani affordable housing project is an initiative by Nairobi City County in partnership with Tecnofin Kenya Limited to renew and re-create Pangani Estate. The NCC aims to provide dignified and affordable homes for residents of Nairobi.

    The Pangani affordable housing project is the premiere PPP (public-private partnership) venture under the Boma Yangu scheme. Construction is ongoing; the Pangani affordable housing project will have 1562 units when complete. In addition, the project includes constructing amenities in a commercial space to provide mixed services to residents.

    Pangani's affordable housing project provides excellent value for money. It is a world-class enclave of convenience, affordability, and modern living.

    2. Starehe affordable housing project

    The Starehe affordable housing project is among the upcoming Boma Yangu projects. Lying approximately 2.5km from the Nairobi CBD, the project is a mixed-use development with social, standard, and accommodation amenities.

    The site is about 24 acres with a development potential of 6,000 units of various mixes of one, two, and three-bedroom accommodation types. The project collaborates with Gulfcap real estate and the government through the State Department for Housing and Urban Development.

    Initially, about 300 houses occupied 24 acres of land before the government relocated the tenants to pave the way for the development. The groundbreaking ceremony is on 6 March 2023, presided by the president.

    3. Mariguini informal settlement

    The Mariguini informal settlement is a slum upgrading project by Nairobi County under the Boma Yangu homes project.

    The site is in Starehe Sub-county, with construction on 6 acres. The proposed Mariguini informal settlement includes 2600 residential units of one, two, and three rooms in high-rise blocks of 10 floors. In addition, the other proposed amenities include a nursery school, dispensary, and community centre.

    4. Kibera Soweto East Zone B

    The government, through the slum upgrading department, is planning the redevelopment of Kibera Soweto East Zone B. The site location is in Langata Sub-county with 4400 units proposed consisting of one, two, and three rooms in high-rise blocks of 12 floors.

    The two-roomed units (one bedroom) will have a shared water closet, shower, and a kitchenette, while the one-roomed units (bedsitter) will have a shared water closet with a shower and a kitchen.

    5. Park Road Ngara

    Park Road Ngara is the first development the government promotes under the affordable housing program. This integrated human settlement project in the Ngara area of the City of Nairobi comprises 1,370 units under the Boma Yangu Ngara housing scheme.

    The project has two sections, category one and two types of accommodation units. Class one consists of two bedrooms with a plinth area of 60 meters square and three with a plinth area of 80 meters square. Category two consists of one bedroom with a plinth area of 30 meters square, two bedrooms with 40 metres square, and three with 60 metres square plinth areas.

    Park Road Ngara's standard and social amenities include parking, a children's playground, a running track, a perimeter wall/security gate, a retail/commercial centre, a lift, and solar water heaters.

    6. Shauri Moyo

    The Shauri Moyo affordable housing project was launched on 27 January 2023 by the president of Kenya. The site is in the eastern zone of NBO county, about 2 kilometres from Nairobi's CBD. The development will be mixed-use with social, common, and housing amenities.

    The site is approximately 8 acres and has the potential to develop over 4,000 units of various mixes of one-bedroom, two-bedroom, and three-bedroom units. Furthermore, the Shauri Moyo affordable housing project will include a secure perimeter fence, a public park, a Kindergarten, retail spaces, water supply, and gas reticulation provisioning.

    Frequently asked questions

  • What are the government affordable housing projects in Kenya? The Affordable Housing Program (AHP) is a government initiative that is one of the Big 4 Agenda's pillars. This pillar focuses on providing decent and affordable housing to low and middle-income families.
  • What is the price of affordable housing in Kenya? The affordable housing price in Kenya is around kes 2,400 per month for a one-bedroom apartment.
  • Who qualifies for Boma Yangu? Boma Yangu applicants must be Kenyan citizens aged 18 years and above. Must be documented with the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA), have a valid Personal Identification Number (PIN) issued by KRA, and be registered on the BomaYangu portal.
  • Who will build affordable houses? The affordable houses will be made and financed by private developers on public and private land across the country.
  • How does the affordable housing programme differ from other housing schemes in Kenya? The affordable housing programme differs from other housing schemes in Kenya by using private sector funding to facilitate the provision of homes.
  • Where and how do I apply to buy an affordable house? Affordable houses under the programme must first register on the Boma Yangu housing portal. Upon registration, your account will enable you to contribute to the housing fund and select your home preference.
  • How will the houses be allocated? The house awarding is through a fair and transparent system, on a first-come, first-served basis, free of human intervention and contact.

    Everything you need to know about affordable housing in Kenya

    Affordable housing in Nairobi is a project by the government that aims to reduce the accommodation crisis for low to middle-class citizens. The project is ongoing, with various stages complete. In addition, the affordable housing scheme, part of the big four agenda, is available nationwide in Kitui, Machakos, Nakuru, Kiambu, and Mombasa counties.

    Are you looking for a way to finance your affordable housing unit? Tuko.co.ke published an informative article about the Boma Yangu payment plan for cash and bank financing. The Boma Yangu payment plan varies depending on a person's preferences.

    Registering on the Boma Yangu platform allows you to select from various payment plans based on your income level. Furthermore, the amount you contribute is determined by the type of housing or unit you wish to purchase.
