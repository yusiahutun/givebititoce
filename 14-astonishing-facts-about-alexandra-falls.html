    14 Astonishing Facts About Alexandra Falls

    Alexandra Falls, located in the Northwest Territories of Canada, is a natural wonder that captivates visitors with its beauty and grandeur. From its impressive height to its rich ecological significance, there are many astonishing facts to discover about this magnificent waterfall.

    The Tallest Waterfall in the Northwest Territories

    Alexandra Falls stands at an impressive height of 103 feet, making it the tallest waterfall in the Northwest Territories. The sheer force of the cascading water creates a breathtaking spectacle that mesmerizes all who witness it.

    A Part of the South Nahanni River

    Alexandra Falls is part of the South Nahanni River, one of Canada’s most renowned waterways. Flowing through vast canyons and untouched wilderness, the river is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and offers breathtaking views along its entire course.

    Named in Honor of Princess Alexandra

    Alexandra Falls was named after Princess Alexandra, a member of the British royal family. The waterfall serves as a tribute to her visit to Canada in 1929 and recognizes her contributions to the country.

    An Important Habitat for Wildlife

    Surrounded by lush vegetation and pristine landscapes, Alexandra Falls provides a crucial habitat for a diverse range of wildlife. From soaring eagles to playful otters, the area around the falls is teeming with animal life.

    A Popular Destination for Hiking

    Alexandra Falls is a favorite destination for hikers and nature enthusiasts. The surrounding area offers a variety of hiking trails that allow visitors to explore the beauty of the falls and its scenic surroundings.

    The Magnificent Twin Falls

    Alexandra Falls is part of a stunning twin waterfall system, with Louise Falls located just downstream. Together, these two waterfalls create a breathtaking display of nature’s power and beauty.

    An Ideal Spot for Photography

    The picturesque setting of Alexandra Falls makes it a paradise for photographers. From capturing the misty cascades to framing the lush landscape, photographers can unleash their creativity in this awe-inspiring location.

    A Historical Landmark

    Alexandra Falls holds historical significance as it is part of Nahanni National Park Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The area has cultural and archaeological importance, with evidence of human habitation dating back thousands of years.

    The Spectacular Ram Plateau

    Alexandra Falls is located near the Ram Plateau, a vast tundra region with unique geological features. The plateau offers breathtaking panoramic views and the chance to observe the rugged beauty of the surrounding landscape.

    A Source of Clean Energy

    The South Nahanni River, which includes Alexandra Falls, is a vital source of hydroelectric power. The falls contribute to the generation of clean energy, playing a role in sustainable development in the region.

    A Destination for Adventure Seekers

    Alexandra Falls and its surrounding area offer endless opportunities for adventure seekers. From river kayaking to fishing, visitors can indulge in thrilling activities while immersing themselves in the natural wonders of the Northwest Territories.

    Preserving Indigenous Heritage

    Alexandra Falls and its surroundings are located in traditional Dene and Métis territories. The area holds cultural and spiritual significance for the indigenous communities, and efforts are made to preserve and honor their heritage.

    A Gateway to Nahanni National Park

    Alexandra Falls serves as a gateway to Nahanni National Park, one of Canada’s most iconic national parks. This vast wilderness is home to majestic peaks, winding rivers, and unique geological formations, offering visitors a true wilderness experience.

    FAQs

    1. How tall is Alexandra Falls?

    Alexandra Falls stands at an impressive height of approximately 32 meters (105 feet). It’s one of the tallest waterfalls in the region, and its sheer size is a sight to behold.

    2. Can you swim or dive in Alexandra Falls?

    No, swimming or diving in Alexandra Falls is not recommended due to the strong currents and rocky terrain. It’s crucial to prioritize safety when visiting the falls and respect any signage or restrictions in place.

    3. Are there any hiking trails near Alexandra Falls?

    Absolutely! For nature enthusiasts, there are several hiking trails in the area that offer breathtaking views of Alexandra Falls and its surrounding landscape. Make sure to bring appropriate footwear and take necessary precautions before embarking on any hiking adventure.

    4. Can you visit Alexandra Falls year-round?

    Yes, Alexandra Falls is accessible year-round. However, the best time to visit is during the summer months when the water flows are at their peak, providing a more dramatic and powerful display.

    5. Is there an entrance fee to visit Alexandra Falls?

    No, there is no entrance fee to visit Alexandra Falls. It’s a public site that can be enjoyed by all, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.
