    Coco Gauff SLAMS 'hype post' from USTA depicting her and fellow USA tennis stars as Wild Thornberrys

    Coco Gauff described the US Tennis Association's effort to send Americans off to the Australian Open in good spirits as a 'foul' attempt at a 'hype post.' In a post that has since been deleted, the USTA shared artwork drawing their athletes in the style of cartoon television show 'The Wild Thornberrys.'

    30 May 2024, 00:00

    Coco Gauff described the US Tennis Association's effort to send Americans off to the Australian Open in good spirits as a 'foul' attempt at a 'hype post.' 

    In a post that has since been deleted, the USTA shared artwork drawing their athletes in the style of cartoon television show 'The Wild Thornberrys.' 

    The decision quickly backfired as their biggest star openly expressed her displeasure at how she and her fellow representatives were portrayed. 


    'Worst thing I've ever seen,' Gauff wrote on an Instagram story on Sunday. 'Like a caricatures artist just decided to make us all look like hideous looking people.

    'The art style is cool for a cartoon show but not for a hype post. foul.'

    Coco Gauff expressed her displeasure over the USTA's 'hideous' depiction of US tennis stars

    Ahead of the Australian Open, the USTA shared a poster in the style of ''The Wild Thornberrys.'

    The poster featured Gauff, Jessica Pegula, Ben Shelton, Sebastian Korda, Taylor Fritz, Frances Tiafoe, Tommy Paul and Emma Navarro. 

    Although not all who were listed expressed their opinion on the artwork, Pegula echoed Gauff's sentiments about how it completely missed the mark. 

    'Hahahahhaha we are ugly af,' Pegula said on X. 

    After drawing eyes to the artwork's 'hideous' depictions, Gauff went on to name her preference for a cartoon template. 

    'Ya'll I know it is a cartoon but I think I would prefer to be drawn as a Bratz art style and not this. This makes us all look so ugly,' Gauff reiterated. 

    Gauff's stardom erupted in 2023 when she won the US Open on top of several singles titles

    Gauff's stardom reached fever pitch in 2023 when she defeated Maria Sakkari in the final of Washington Open to earn her first WTA 500 singles title. 

    She then followed it up with her first WTA 1000 title at the Cincinnati Open, upsetting No 1 ranked Iga Swiatek in the semifinals before besting No 10 Karonlina Muchova in the finals. 

    To cap off her breakthrough year, Gauff won the 2023 US Open for her first Major singles title. Gauff defeated No 2 Aryna Sabalenka in three sets to become the first teenager to win the tournament since Serena Williams in 1999.

    Gauff's journey to win the Australian open will begin on Sunday when she faces a grizzled veteran in Slovakia's Anna Karolina Schmiedlova.
