    It was a joy for a 29-year-old woman after she got an opportunity to join college 12 years after completing her high school examinations.

    What is KCSE?

    The woman, who sat for her Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) in 2012, could not join college due to a lack of fees and proceeded to get married.

    Vivian Achieng told TUKO.co.ke that she had given up hope, having stayed out of school for a while and having moved on to start a family.

    "I got married and, after two years, got a baby. I tried to join an early childhood education training college but deferred shortly after because I could not raise the required fees," she recounted.

    However, when she thought her dream of a vocation was over, an opportunity presented itself, and she decided to take it.

    Does Homa Bay county sponsor vocational training?

    The resident of Homa Bay said that she learned about the county's programme to support students who needed vocational training to join college and seized the opportunity to express interest and apply for sponsorship.

    This time, God's favour was upon her as she was selected among 600 students to be sponsored for training under the county's KSh 24 million Tujiajiri Fundi Mang'ula Scholarship programme.

    Gladys Wanga, the Homa Bay governor, said the programme aims to empower youths in the county to acquire competitive technical skills and employ themselves and others.

    Kenya has been facing a shortage of skilled labourers in plumbing, electrical wiring, and related courses after most learners prioritised blue-collar courses in colleges and universities.

    Shortage of plumbers and electricians in Kenya

    The late George Magoha, former Education Cabinet secretary, once narrated how he had trouble finding a plumber in his neighbourhood and emphasised the need for such a skilled workforce.

    Subscribe to watch new videos"I embrace this opportunity with everything that I am. This is one sure chance for me to continue my education after 12 years since I sat for KCSE. It's a privilege for me, and I cannot take it for granted. It's an answered prayer," she said.

    Achieng's desire to get a vocation is clear in her story.

    "I tried teaching at a local ECDE centre as a Parents Teachers Association employee to make ends meet but I could not do much because I did not have credentials for permanent employment and better pay," she stated.

    This was after she dropped out of an ECDE centre shortly after getting admission but with no funds to stay in school.

    Since then, she has made ends meet by volunteering in different organisations.

    Vocational training colleges in Homa Bay

    "During my volunteer work at one of the organisations, a colleague told me about the Tujiajiri Fundi Mang'ula Scholarship. The colleague urged me to apply knowing that I had given up. I applied and after some time I was notified I was shortlisted for a interview which I subsequently passed," she narrated.

    She told TUKO.co.ke that she had secured a spot at Sero Vocational Training College, where she is pursuing a six-month food and beverage course.

    "This is an opportunity to start afresh, and I promise I will be the best in my class because this is what I have been yearning for. I have the passion and determination to do it and change my life's story," she passionately assured.

    KCB sponsors Homa Bay youths to join vocational colleges

    Sponsoring youths to study vocational courses is part of Homa Bay's manifesto.

    The county set aside KSh 12 million for the programme, and Kenya Commercial Bank added another KSh 12 million to make the scholarship kitty KSh 24 million.

    Wanga said that the programme covers 600 youths, 15 from each of the 40 wards in the county.

    "By arming our young people with the necessary technical skills, we are not only helping them secure employment opportunities but also preparing them to become future entrepreneurs and job creators," Wanga said.

    She urged the beneficiaries to do their best to reciprocate with the taxpayers and sponsors.

    Wanga has been lauded for championing unique life-transformative programmes like the international conference that President William Ruto graced.
