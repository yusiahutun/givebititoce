    Catherine, Princess of Wales, paid tribute to the late Princess Diana through her gorgeous look for last night's Earthshot Prize Awards.

    For the last engagement of her and Prince William's jam-packed Boston tour, the princess chose a bright, mint-green off-the-shoulder gown by Solace London, which she rented from the British platform HURR. She paired the jaw-dropping dress with a special accessory: an emerald-and-diamond choker necklace which previously belonged to her late mother-in-law.

    Karwai Tang//Getty Images

    The opulent necklace was gifted to Diana by Queen Elizabeth, following her wedding to Prince Charles. During the pair's 1985 Australia tour, the late princess memorably wore the piece as a headband. It was paired with a teal-green one-shouldered ballgown, which featured a drop-waist and a large bow decoration.

    Anwar Hussein//Getty Images

    Diana also wore the piece again for an engagement at the Dorchester Hotel in 1993, when it accompanied a bright green column gown with wrap detailing and a subtle off-the-shoulder silhouette.

    Tim Graham//Getty Images

    Kate's glamorous appearance for the Friday night prize ceremony concluded the Prince and Princess of Wales' three-day trip, which marked their first visit to the U.S. since 2014. The princess' fashion for the tour included several elegant suits, including a sleek all-navy ensemble for the couple's arrival at Boston's Logan International Airport, and a wine-colored set paired with a pink pussy-bow blouse for a visit to Greentown Labs in Somerville, Massachusetts.

    Kate also took part in a solo outing to Harvard's Center on the Developing Child, which has a relationship with her The Royal Foundation Centre for Early Childhood. For the visit, the royal wore a bold houndstooth midi dress in black and blue from Emilia Wickstead, paired with black Gianvito Rossi suede pumps and gold Liv Thurlwell hoop earrings.
