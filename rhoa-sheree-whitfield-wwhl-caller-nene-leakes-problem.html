    "Real Housewives of Atlanta" star Shereé Whitfield gave one of the sassiest "Watch What Happens Live" interviews in a long time, and we are here for it.

    All season long, Shereé has been open about sharing her relationship with incarcerated boyfriend Tyrone Gilliams with Bravo cameras, allowing their phone conversations to be filmed.

    "I saw that you got a call from your honey, Tyrone," a caller asked the "RHOA" star Sunday night. "He was telling you all these things that he was going to do for you when he gets out of jail, and I was just curious, financially, how is he gonna be able to do these things? Does he still have money that he's kept from his embezzling days?"

    "Ohhh, his embezzling days?" Shereé said, visibly taken aback. "He's definitely OK, so if I'm not worried about it, you probably shouldn't be either."

    A caller then asked Shereé if she thought RoachGate was "overrated" given that she, too, had a roach on her back porch in a scene from an episode this season.

    "Outside!" she fired back. "I didn't have a colony inside."

    A third caller asked Shereé why all the women on the show kiss NeNe Leakes' ass.

    "Wait, she said all the ladies are kissing NeNe's butt?" Shereé asked host Andy Cohen for clarification. "No, I've never been a butt-kisser, and that's probably the problem between me and NeNe."

    Although Shereé says she's not one of them, she does think NeNe has "quite a few puppets."

    Andy then read a tweet from a viewer who said watching Shereé defend Kim Zolciak and her daughter, Brielle Biermann, regarding the roach video the latter took in NeNe's bathroom was "disgusting."

    "Listen, I'm not justifying their actions," Shereé said. "I told Kim that I didn't think it was appropriate for Brielle to be in someone's house taping, recording; however, I am not here to fight NeNe's battles or anyone else's because they've never fought mine."

    Shereé then went in on NeNe's apology after the latter told a heckler at a standup comedy show she was performing at that she hoped they got raped by their Uber driver on their way home.

    "I just wasn't buying it," Shereé said, regarding the apology. "To me, to even utter anything like that out of your mouth when you say you're a victim of domestic violence, is just ridiculous for me. It's disgusting."
