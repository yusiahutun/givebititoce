    She is known for her age-defying looks and luscious blonde locks.

    And while Australian supermodel Elle Macpherson has rocked long hair extensions for years, she has now decided to change up her look.

    Last weekend, the WelleCo founder, 59, flaunted her new shorter and blonder hair in an Instagram photo, and said she is now keeping better care of her locks after ditching the extensions.

    While she regularly bleached and used heat on her hair during her long-standing modelling career, Elle has learned how to protect her hair when she's not working from the best in the beauty business.

    From ditching the hot tools to protecting her locks from the harsh sun, Elle has shared her top five tips for optimal health hair on her Get The Gloss column. 

      Elle Macpherson has flaunted her new shorter and blonder hair in an Instagram photo, and said she is now keeping better care of her locks after ditching the extensions

    1. Eat more nutrient food and supplement where needed

    Elle is a big believer eating well and having a healthy gut plays a crucial role in keeping hair thick and luscious.

    'The gut plays a crucial role in nutrient absorption,' she said.

    'The combination of botanical herbs, antioxidants, vitamins, minerals, and probiotics that feature in The Super Elixir™ and The Hair Elixir [from WelleCo] will keep your gut microbiome, and in turn your hair, healthy.'

    2. Find a good hairdresser who is passionate about hair health

    The global supermodel has talented stylists around the world she can rely on, including London-based Josh Wood and Miami stylist Dafne Evangelista.

    Throughout her decades-long career, Elle learned from many stylists both on and off set.

    While she regularly bleached and used heat on her hair during her long-standing modelling career, Elle has learned how to protect her hair when she's not working from the best in the beauty business 

    3. Put the hot tools down

    'Natural is always best,' Elle continued.

    'Your hair will thank you for avoiding hot styling tools when you don’t need them.'

    The supermodel said she only uses hot tools when she works and keeps it natural for the rest of the time.

    4. Sun protection isn't just for your skin

    As she often spends time outdoors, Elle makes sure to protect her hair from the sun and believes this is key to keeping her hair silky and healthy.

    When she is out in the sun and the sea, she uses hydrating and protecting masks.

    In colder climates, the supermodel wears her hair in braids with a leave-in mask to ensure UV protection and hydration.

    Elle is a big believer that eating well and having a healthy gut plays a crucial role in keeping hair thick and luscious

    5. Don't forget about scalp health

    One of Elle's biggest tips for healthier hair is a well-looked-after scalp.

    She likes to give herself a gentle scalp massage with a natural oil like coconut, almond, or jojoba to improve blood circulation and stimulate hair growth.
