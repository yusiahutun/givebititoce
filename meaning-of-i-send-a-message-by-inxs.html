    Meaning of I Send A Message by INXS

    13 Mar 2024, 00:00

    In the world of music, some songs have the power to transport us to different times and places, evoking emotions and thoughts that we might have buried deep within our souls. One such song is INXS' "I Send a Message," a beautiful composition that resonates with listeners on multiple levels. The song, released in 1984, explores the themes of longing, distance, and connection, all wrapped up in a powerful melody that captures the essence of human relationships.The opening line, "In the silence, I think of you," immediately sets the tone for the song. It speaks to the universal experience of missing someone, whether they are physically distant or emotionally unavailable. Here, the theme of longing begins to unfold, highlighting the human need for connection and the power that others hold over our thoughts and emotions."I send a message, and I hope it gets through" is a lyric that further develops the theme of connection. It delves into the realm of uncertainty and vulnerability, as the singer hopes for a reciprocation of feelings. This lyric conveys the power of communication in maintaining relationships, both romantic and platonic. It reminds us that expressing our emotions openly and honestly can bridge the gap between people, even when physical distance separates them.The line "Think of the distance, think of the miles, all of the valleys, could take a while" delves into the idea of physical separation and the challenges it brings. It highlights the obstacles faced when trying to maintain a connection with someone who is far away. The theme of distance is further emphasized, illustrating the struggles and longing that come with prolonged separation.As the song progresses, a sense of nostalgia and yearning is conveyed through the lyric "I miss the people, I miss the fun." This line not only speaks to missing specific individuals but also hints at a deeper longing for a time when life was simpler and carefree. It suggests that personal connections and shared experiences are what truly give meaning to our lives.The chorus, "You're my apparition, she's my only one," introduces a mysterious and ethereal element to the song. The theme of apparitions brings to mind the idea of ghostly presences, lingering memories, and the ways in which people can haunt our thoughts and emotions. This theme adds a layer of complexity to the song, exploring the inner workings of the human mind, where memories and desires converge.The lyric "Man of the world for all the good reasons, take away the pain and drink the wine" introduces a theme of escapism and self-reflection. It portrays the longing for a better, more fulfilling life and suggests that there are ways to temporarily transcend our problems through indulgence and self-discovery. This theme speaks to the human desire for freedom and happiness while acknowledging the complex realities that often hold us back.Throughout the song, there is a recurring motif of silence, which adds a poignant layer to the overarching themes. Silence represents the unspoken, the thoughts and emotions that we keep hidden. It suggests an inner dialogue between the singer and the person they long for, a desire for the unexpressed to be heard and understood. This motif deepens our understanding of the struggle to communicate in relationships, and the profound impact that silence can have."I Send a Message" is a song that explores the universal human experience of longing, distance, and connection. Through its thought-provoking lyrics, it takes listeners on a journey of self-reflection and inner exploration. It reminds us of the power that others hold over our thoughts and emotions and highlights the importance of open communication in bridging distances, both physical and emotional. Ultimately, the song invites us to reflect on the connections that shape our lives, reminding us of the beauty and complexity of the human experience.
