    Imagine your skin as a delicate ecosystem teeming with a variety of good and bad bacteria. Keeping the right balance is crucial for healthy skin. However, regular cleansers often disrupt this balance, causing problems like inflammation, dryness, and breakouts. That's where the best low pH cleansers swoop in to save the day! With their carefully formulated ingredients, these cleansers work synergistically with your skin's natural acid mantle, shielding it from external aggressors.

    The physiological pH of the human body plays a pivotal role in a majority of processes necessary to life. In human’s organs, the pH level varies from 1 to 8. To be more precise, the outermost layer of the epidermis or stratum corneum’s pH ranges between 4.1 to 5.8 (1). On an average, the pH value of the skin’s surface is 4.7 (2). It is thus important to invest in skincare products that help you maintain the natural pH level of your skin. Our contributor Dr. Hamdan, an experienced dermatologist says, “Skin over different parts of the body has different pH levels. So to maintain the pH levels of the skin, the first thing you need to do is to identify which soap or cleanser will be ideal for the alkaline or acidity level.”

    Today, in addition to cleansers, there is also a wide range of low-pH soaps or pH-balancing face washes available in the market. All of these skincare products are designed to balance your skin’s natural pH levels, maintain the efficacy of the barriers, and prevent over-drying of the skin. Cleansers, in specific, generally feature a gentle, mild, and skin-soothing formula that indirectly helps in taking good care of your skin. Our contributor Dr. Hamdan says, “pH level 7 is considered the neutral level and anything under that would count as acidic. Maintaining the pH levels is important to overall skin health and if you maintain it, you can fight dangerous microbes that will affect the aging process of your skin.” While talking about cleansing and its effects Dr. Surbhi Mahajan, an Aesthetic and Pediatric Dermatologist says, “Cleansing should always be mediocre so that it doesn’t strip off the natural moisturizing factors of skin and disturb the skin barrier. However, over-cleansing can increase acne and pigmentation.” For all those who wish to know more about cleansers, scroll down and check out our list of the top 7 cleansers that you should invest in.

    What to Consider When Buying a Low pH Cleanser?

    Here are a few aspects that you need to consider while purchasing some good quality low ph facial cleansers for everyday use. 

    Skin Type: Always consider your skin type before splurging on a low ph face wash or ph balanced facial cleanser. Choose a product that is specially formulated for your skin type. Some cleansers are specially crafted for oily, sensitive, dry, or acne-prone skin whereas some are meant for all skin types. To prevent any reaction, pick one that is meant for your skin only. People with sensitive skin should vouch for a gentle cleanser whereas the ones with oily or combination skin can rely on a sebum-balancing cleanser. Lastly, dry skin folks can invest in moisturizing or hydrating cleansers.  

    Ingredients: Paying heed to the list of ingredients is of utmost importance especially when your skin reacts immediately to them or requires additional nourishment. Those possessing dry skin must choose a low-pH cleanser with moisturizing and hydrating ingredients like hyaluronic acid. On the other hand, people dealing with oily skin should rely on cleansers that are infused with oil-controlling or oil-free ingredients. Lastly, the ones with sensitive skin should choose a cleanser that contains non-toxic and non-irritating ingredients. Moreover, avoiding chemical-infused cleansers is always advisable. 

    pH Levels: Make sure you check the pH levels of the cleanser that you are planning to buy. This information might help you to analyze the results well in advance. Products ranging between 5.5 to 6.5 are highly recommended. They help in maintaining your skin’s acid mantle.

    Formula: Ensure that your cleanser has a clean formula and contains no harmful or chemical-based ingredients as they might worsen your skin concerns (if any). Always pick a ph balanced cleanser for the face that is cruelty, paraben, and sulfate free.  

    Type of Cleanser: Various types of cleansers can be distinguished as per their texture or consistency. Cleansers are available in gel, water, foam, and cream-based textures. Each of them features distinct properties to serve what your skin type needs. Foam-based cleansers generally lather well and suit oily skin as they help in washing off deeply seated impurities, grime, and control oil. On the other hand, cream-based cleansers are more nourishing and gentle on the skin.   

    After taking note of these 5 essential aspects, roll your eyes over and learn how to use the best low pH cleanser for desired results.

    How to Use a Low pH Cleanser?

    Following are steps to use a high-grade and well-crafted ph face cleanser for smooth and squeaky clean skin. 

    Step 1: Use a cleanser in the morning to clean your face and always take off your applied makeup before cleansing your face at night. You can use makeup removers, cleansing wipes, or cleansing water for easy removal of the applied layer. 

    Step 2: Wet your face. Pour a sufficient quality cleanser on your palms. Begin applying the cleanser. Do not forget your neck or near-the-ear areas. 

    Step 3: Gently massage your face in circular motions for a minimum of 25 to 40 seconds. Let your cleanser lather well. Focus on areas like the neck, jawline, ears, nose, upper lips, etc. 

    Step 4: Rinse your face with lukewarm water and pat dry with a microfibre towel.

    Step 5: Apply a moisturizer or serum to moisturize your skin.

    FAQ's

    Is a low pH cleanser good for sensitive skin?

    Yes. Low pH cleansers do not dry or irritate sensitive skin.

    Who should use a low pH cleanser?

    People dealing with rosacea, acne, and very sensitive skin should use low pH cleansers.

    Are high pH cleansers bad for skin?

    High pH cleansers can be harsh on the skin's natural barrier, stripping away its protective oils and causing dryness and irritation. It's important to use a pH-balanced cleanser to maintain healthy skin.

    How can you know when a cleanser has a low pH?

    When a cleanser has a low pH, it can be indicated by its mildness on the skin, as it helps to maintain the skin's natural acidic barrier, leaving it feeling refreshed and balanced. Additionally, looking for key ingredients such as butylene glycol, citric acid or lactic acid on the product label can also signal a low pH cleanser.
