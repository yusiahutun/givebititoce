    Mombasa Man Recalls Woman Predicting He'd Die Next at Dad Burial: &quot;Degree Yako Itaenda Mchangani&quot;

    Felix from Mombasa has claimed that a strange woman said he was next to die after his father, and since then, he has known no peace.

    Felix's mother dies in December 1995

    The young man grew up in a happy family, and all was well until things came tumbling down owing to his mother's death on December 17, 1995.

    "My stepmum came the following year, and initially, things were just okay; everything changed when she was unable to get children," said Felix. "It is in the family domain that she has never had a good relationship with my sister and I. My dad was very principled, and he did not want any games with his children," he added.

    On November 15, 2020, Felix's dad fell sick; initially, he was suspected to have COVID-19 and had to be isolated at the hospital. When Felix's dad was being prepared for treatment, he asked for his son, but they never told the young man who was just waiting outside the hospital.

    Strange woman predicts Felix's death

    Prior to his dad's death, Felix claims that his stepmother used to block him and even prevent him from getting money from his dad. Ultimately, Felix's dad died without seeing his son; they only used to communicate through the hospital's system.

    When they arrived with his dad's body at home, it was pitch dark, and the strangest thing was that the power blackout had only happened in their home.

    "As I was ensuring everything was okay, the voice of a lady came to my ear and said you see, a degree has gone into the soil, yours is coming soon," said Felix. "When I turned, I saw a woman melt into the shadows," he added.

    Despite the strange encounter, Felix and his family laid their dad to rest. At the funeral, the Kenya Ports Authority personnel announced that they were going to give him work since his dad had been their employer.

    Voices in Felix's head

    Apparently, it was a huge mistake announcing the matter in front of everyone as Felix never knew peace from that day.

    "I used to hear voices and even took up a lot of drinking; I would sell everything in the house after my wife went to work," he shared.

    Christabel, Felix's wife, shared that he struggled with alcohol, and sometimes, he wanted to kill her.

    "He said the voices used to tell him to kill me because I was a prayer warrior. I never used to welcome people to my house for fear that they would advise me wrongly about leaving him," she said. "I wanted to take him back home, but his sister said that would kill him. According to a pastor who prayed for him, someone had used witchcraft on Felix because the day his dad died, Kenya Ports Authority had offered him a job, and this didn't go well with people who had attended the funeral," Christabel explained.

    Christabel stands by Felix

    Many would mock them, especially when he used to pick papers. He denied pregnancy because his stepmother told him he would never have a home or a child.

    "We have really visited churches, but it didn't help. He even used to beat me up while I was pregnant, and whenever he brought papers home, he would insist it was shopping and beat me when I contradicted him," shared the devoted wife.

    A pastor encouraged Christabel to leave her husband, but she left the church instead.

    One day, after his wife left their door ajar, Felix went to church, and after the preaching, pastor Joseph of Jesus Winner Ministry said God would heal him.

    "After praying for me, the pastor said the best thing would be to hide me at the pulpit. He took me to Bishop Mwai's crusade in Kasarani and prayed for me," said Felix.

    Felix is now healed, doing well for himself and preaching at the church. Despite his degree in social work, he has yet to get a job.

    Christabel pleads with Kenyans to help Felix

    "My husband applies for jobs, but he has never found any. I sell water to make ends meet, and our pastor gives us some money because he knows we are not earning much," said Christabel.

    From being mentally challenged, Felix is now a preacher and pastor Joseph is grooming him slowly. He is eternally grateful to his wife for standing by him through thick and thin.

    "Sincerely speaking, you have played everything for me to be here today. Let me assure you that I love and Iam grateful to you for everything you have done for me; I will love you forever," said Felix. "I feel it is now my turn to take care of our family," he said.

    On her part, Christabel pleaded with Kenyans to give her husband a job as he has been tarmacking for a long time.

    "I love you so much, my husband; people have told me to leave you, but my heart will always love you. I pray that God will give you a job so that you can go to work, be like other husbands and change our lives," she she told TUKO.co.ke in an inteview.
