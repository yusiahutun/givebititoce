    Channing Tatum Made Heartwarming Comment About Zo Kravitz

    If you thought Channing Tatum and Zo Kravitz couldn't get any cuter, think again. The actor just unlocked new levels of adorableness by opening up about what it was like to work with his now-fiance on their upcoming movie Blink Twice (formerly Pussy Island), coming to theaters Aug. 23.

    11 Apr 2024, 00:00

    If you thought Channing Tatum and Zoë Kravitz couldn't get any cuter, think again. The actor just unlocked new levels of adorableness by opening up about what it was like to work with his now-fiancée on their upcoming movie Blink Twice (formerly Pussy Island), coming to theaters Aug. 23.

    Tatum shared a promo photo to Instagram on Monday that features a series of Polaroid pictures splattered with blood with the words, "Make memories ... Tomorrow #blinktwicemovie" written along the bottom.

    "I have so much that I can’t wait to share because this project is near and dear to my heart in more ways than I can count," he captioned the post, presumably referring to the fact that he met his future wife on set.

    The film, which is co-written and directed by Kravitz, also stars Naomi Ackie, Geena Davis, Alia Shawkat, Simon Rex, Haley Joel Osment, Christian Slater, and Kyle MacLachlan. According to People, the movie follows a billionaire (played by Tatum) who invites his friends to a "mysterious private island."

    Kravitz also shared a movie poster on her Instagram with different Polaroids of each character with the same chilling statement scrawled across the bottom. Kravitz also revealed that the film's trailer will drop tomorrow, Apr. 23.

    "Here. we. go. @blinktwicemovie trailer. tomorrow," she wrote.

    Tatum previously revealed in Kravitz's WSJ. Magazine story that the two met through the film, and similar to their relationship, the project has evolved over time. "When we first met the movie was pretty different than its form now, but the themes were the same," he said at the time. "All the iterations it has gone through were all pretty punk rock, to be honest."

    In the same interview, Kravitz said that working together was a bonding experience for the couple. “When you make things with people, it’s a very sacred space, and when you’re compatible with somebody creatively, it often opens up other channels because you’re kind of sharing all of yourself,” she said at the time. “I’m really grateful that this movie has brought him into my life that way.”

    Kravitz also called him her "protector" on set in the GQ 2022 Men of the Year issue. "Whether it was making me tea or pouring me a drink or going to whip someone into shape or whatever — he really was my protector, and it was really wonderful and sweet," she recalled. "I think if you can do something like that together, it's a good test. And we came out even stronger."
