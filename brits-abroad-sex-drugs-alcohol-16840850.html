    Ski village turned into Magaluf-on-Ice as Brits get messy at Bulgarian resort

    The tiny mountain town of Borovets has become a magnet for randy revellers. A Daily Star investigation found the village in Bulgaria is a hotbed for stag parties drawn in by strip clubs and cheap alcohol.

    31 Jul 2024, 00:00

    The tiny mountain town of Borovets has become a magnet for randy revellers.

    A Daily Star investigation found the village in Bulgaria is a hotbed for stag parties drawn in by strip clubs and cheap alcohol.

    Our reporter found women and drugs being openly sold on the streets, with one tout for its adult venues forcing fliers into the hands of drunk lads.

    He told us: “Do you want a good time?

    “I can guarantee you can get anything you ask for in this club.

    “In the morning I sell pancakes to children, and in the night I sell drugs and women to adults.”

    He then offered to sell a gram of cannabis to the Daily Star for £15.

    One British reveller told us: “We went into one of the strip clubs to see what it was like.

    “But it was horrible – and immediately the women offered us sex – we couldn’t believe they were so open about it.

    “It is weird – there are kids here and families around – but it is such good value in a resort – so much cheaper than Austria or France.”

    His friend added: “It was just really depressing.”

    Nearly 400,000 Brit skiers and snow-boarders plan on getting boozy on the slopes this year, research has revealed.

    Over the course of a week’s holiday, Brits who “drink-ski” will consume 105 units of alcohol, the equivalent of 53 pints of beer or glasses of wine.

    And according to the poll by Direct Line, 61% say consuming alcohol on the slopes is sociable and is not dangerous.

    Our reporter also witnessed one British stag group stripping down to their bare chests in the sub-zero temperatures.

    One bar worker who watched the bizarre spectacle unfold told us: “British people definitely do get the most drunk, I have seen so many things like this.”

    Another Brit on a family trip said: “It really is Magaluf-on-Ice. You see some sights in the bars – it is a real party town.”
