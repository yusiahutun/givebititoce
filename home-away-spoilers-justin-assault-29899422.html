    Desperate to get a hold of her, Theo sends Ava a sorry message saying he made a mistake and wants to know if she’s okay.

    Reading the message the wrong way, Ava uses it to her advantage and replies that she’s in trouble.

    After forwarding the message onto Justin, he finds his daughter lying on a mattress in the back of a shirtless stranger’s combi.

    Rushing over to the stranger, Justin punches a clueless Conor in the face in front of Ava.

    With the police arriving on scene, an enraged Justin is pulled off a severely beaten Conor.

    At the police station, as Justin is dragged into an interview room, a distraught Ava tells Theo he was meant to come, not her dad.

    Meanwhile, Leah returns to the Bay and rushes to the police station.

    In the interview room, Justin is worried that he was too late to stop Ava from being sexually assaulted.

    After Ava is taken home, the guilt becomes too much and she admits that Conor didn’t do anything wrong.

    Upon learning Ava wasn't harmed, Justin's elation quickly turns to regret when he realises he assaulted an innocent man.

    The police charge him with grievous bodily harm before he is let out on bail and heads to confronts Ava.

    Trying to avoid confrontation, Ava packs her bags to leave but Justin challenges her.

    The teenager admits that for once she wanted someone to put her first and how she feels Justin was never there for her.

    Realising Justin could go to prison, Ava apologises and the two become determined to get to know each other better.

    While Ava and Justin have forgiven each other, the same can’t be said for Leah.

    Elsewhere, Remi admits to Felicity that Bree saw another woman leaving his house but he was too caught up in his own pride and didn’t tell her it was only platonic.

    As Bree struggles with her upcoming abortion, Rose suggests she should tell Remi.

    Later, Remi admits to Bree that nothing happened with that girl, prompting her to reveal that she is pregnant.

    As a shell-shocked Remi digests the news that Bree is pregnant, she reveals she doesn't know who's baby it is.

    Remi reassures her that she has nothing to be ashamed about, but it's all to much for Bree who makes her getaway when the conversation is interrupted.

    When he tracks Bree down, she admits she’s getting an abortion tomorrow.

    With a little push from Eden, a stoic Remi finds Bree and tells her he’s going to be by her side at the appointment.

    Meanwhile, sick of waiting for Cash and Eden t o get back to them with wedding plans, Felicity and Tane decide to get married the next day.

    With the wedding being a small event, the decision is made to have a party at Salt.

    After overhearing Felicity practising her vows in te reo Māori, Tane calls Cash to help him with some special plans for the wedding.

    Responding to an SOS, Kirby and Theo come back from their camping trip early to play at the wedding.

    As Felicity is chaotically trying to pick out a wedding dress, Eden’s surprised to find Cash’s wedding ring he bought for Jasmine.

    Later, Cash asks to walk Felicity down the aisle which she happily accepts.

    Felicity is happily stunned when she arrives at her wedding to discover Tane, wearing a ceremonial Korowai, has organised a traditional Māori wedding with a Kaikaranga and traditional poi dancers.

    As Felicity recites her vows to Tane in te reo Māori, he wraps his Korowai around her, symbolising his love that will surround and protect her for the rest of their lives.

    Meanwhile, Bree thanks Remi as he's by her side during her abortion.

    Afterwards, Bree forces Remi to play at the wedding afterparty.

    While he doesn’t want to leave her side, she kisses him to show her appreciation and acceptance.

    Remi arrives last minute and plays at the wedding reception.

    The next day, Cash wakes up very hungover. A trail of his and Eden’s clothes leading from the living room to the bedroom.

    Most shocking for Cash – Eden is now wearing the wedding ring he bought Jasmine – did he propose to Eden last night?

    Across the Bay, John returns to discover Dean and Ziggy have left without saying goodbye.

    Feeling like an afterthought, an insecure and hurt John turns his frustration towards Mali.

    When Mali goes into his office, he finds John sitting despondently while a scrawled goodbye message from Dean and Ziggy is written on the whiteboard.

    Knowing how hurt John is, Mali brings him coffee and then pizza to try and lift his spirits.

    But the pain is still too raw, and John pushes him away both times.

    Later, when Mali returns to drop off the left-over pizza, John is more receptive and asks him to join him as a mate.

    John apologises for how he acted, he was lashing out as he thought he meant more to Dean then a hastily written note.
