    The Six Million Dollar Man's main cast comprised actors Lee Majors, Richard Anderson, Martin E. Brooks, Lindsay Wagner, and Alan Oppenheimer. But what happened to them after the show ended in 1978? Are they all alive?

    1. Lee Majors

    • Full name: Harvey Lee Yeary
    • Date of birth: April 23, 1939
    • Place of birth: Wyandotte, Michigan, USA

    Lee Majors is an American actor. He played the character of Colonel Steve Austin, a former astronaut who, after a devastating accident, is transformed into a bionic man by the government. The character became a cultural icon, propelling Lee Majors to stardom.

    Majors has had other roles since he appeared in The Six Million Dollar Man. He portrayed the characters of Heath Barkley on the American television Western series The Big Valley (1965–1969) and Colt Seavers on the American television action series The Fall Guy (1981–1986).

    2. Richard Anderson

    • Full name: Richard Norman Anderson
    • Date of birth: August 8, 1926
    • Place of birth: Long Branch, New Jersey, USA

    Richard Norman Anderson was an American film and television actor. He is well known for his roles as Oscar Goldman, the boss of Steve Austin in The Six Million Dollar Man. He also played the same role in The Bionic Woman television series between 1974 and 1978 and their subsequent television movies.

    According to IMDb, Richard had 192 acting credits. Some of his notable film and TV roles include Paths of Glory, Perry Mason, The Wild, Wild West Bonanza and more. Anderson died on August 31 2017, aged 91, from natural causes in Beverly Hills, California.

    Was Sandra Bullock in The Six Million Dollar Man?

    Yes, Sandra Bullock appeared in the made-for-television movie version of The Six Million Dollar Man, but not in the original series. According to IMDb, the actress played the role of Kate Mason in Bionic Showdown: The Six Million Dollar Man and the Bionic Woman, which aired on April 30 1989.

    Why did The Six Million Dollar Man get cancelled?

    The show was cancelled on March 6, 1978, after five seasons and 99 episodes because of poor ratings. In addition, the comic book and magazine versions were also cancelled in June 1978 and November 1977, respectively.
