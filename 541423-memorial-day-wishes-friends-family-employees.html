    Memorial Day, founded in 1868, was originally for celebrating Confederate and Union soldiers who died in the Civil War. It now represents all Americans who lost their lives in war and is often referred to as "Decoration Day" because of the tradition of visiting loved ones' gravesites to leave flags.

    Is it right to say Happy Memorial Day?

    While it is a common greeting, "Happy Memorial Day" is usually received with mixed reactions. Memorial Day honours fallen heroes and is thus a sad occasion. Therefore, "happy" might feel out of place and insensitive, more so to those who lost their loved ones in service.
