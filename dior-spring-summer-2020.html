    For Dior spring/summer 2020, Maria Grazia Chiuri created an "inclusive garden" that was inspired by archival photographs of Catherine Dior, Christian Dior's sister, showing her surrounded by flowers in her garden, which was her passion. Catherine – whose nickname was Miss Dior and is who the house's first fragrance was named after – was an essential protagonist in the fashion house's history.

    The impact of this inspiration and of Catherine Dior's love of gardening specifically was felt throughout the collection (on floral-embroidered pieces, straw hats and textured raffia dresses) and through the impressive set, which included more than 100 trees.

    And, while some brands face criticism for their temporary show sets, Dior combatted this by donating every single tree that was used in the show to various community garden projects. The brand used the hashtag #PlantingForTheFuture to promote its plan, but guests could also scan a QR Code on each of the trees to find out where it will be planted.

    The spring/summer 2020 show was attended by a whole host of famous fashion faces, including Jennifer Lawrence, Julianne Moore, Natalia Vodianova, Erin O'Connor and Karlie Kloss.

    "These trees are being replanted," says Kloss in the video above. "I think that's the kind of consciousness and approach that the fashion industry needs to continue to take."
