    'They had everything to live for': Mother's grief after 'murder-suicide' deaths of her son and his family as it's revealed business exec bought fireworks just days before fire engulfed James Blake's mansion

    • The bodies of Darrin Campbell, his wife Kimberly, and their teenage children Colin and Megan were discovered inside the Tampa mansion that erupted early Wednesday
    • But detectives believe the family died in a murder suicide prior to the blaze
    • Mr Campbell's mother, Mary Campbell, also told MailOnline her son, 51, had just landed a new job after being unemployed for sometime
    • The family had been renting the property from retired tennis pro James Blake for two years

    A business executive who died with his wife and teenage children in a murder-suicide at a Florida mansion had 'everything to live for' and had recently landed a new job, his mother told MailOnline.

    The mysterious development comes as it's revealed Darrin Campbell, 51, was the one who bought the fireworks that reportedly surrounded the bodies and apparently started the fire that engulfed the two story, five bedroom home the family was leasing from tennis pro James Blake.

    Mr Campbell was found dead in the burnt out $1.5 million home alongside his wife Kimberly.

    His two teenage children Colin, 19, and Megan, 16, were found in their respective bedrooms - with police saying two of the victims had suffered 'upper body trauma.'

    Scroll down for audio and video

    Remains: This aerial photo shows the burned out home owned by James Blake on Thursday, May 8, a day after it was set alight

    Sinister: Fireworks were found strewn around the house and police say an accelerant - probably the gas canisters he bought - were used to start the blaze

    Police refused to elaborate on the 'upper body trauma' but sources said the terms was usually applied to gunshot or knife wounds.

    While the exact cause of death of the family of four is not yet known police in suspect a murder-suicide and said they are not looking for any other suspects.

    In previous murder-suicides across the U.S. it is usually the husband who kills his family before taking his own life although earlier this year a Florida mum killed her two children at their Palm Beach home before taking her own life.

    The Hillsborough County Medical Examiner's Office is carrying out the autopsy on the four victims with the results expected to be known later today. A spokesman for Hillsborough County Sheriff's Office said a team of 10 investigators were involved in probing the fatal fire.

    Police said an accelerant had been used to spread the fire which quickly gutted the mansion more than 6,000 sqft mansion.

    In an exclusive interview with Mail Online Campbell's heartbroken mother said the family are at a loss to know what took place inside the home.

    Victim: Darrin Campbell, pictured right, his wife Kimberly and their teenage children aged 19 and 16 were identified as the victims of the fire that engulfed the home they were renting from tennis ace James Blake, left

    Charred: The house owned by former tennis pro James Blake is seen after Wednesday's fire



    Mary Campbell said her son had recently landed a new job and was looking forward to starting work while her grandson Colin was due to graduate from his private school in a month.

    'They were a perfect family and everything was just right,' said 79 year old Mrs Campbell. 'I spoke to Darrin the other night and everything was just fine. We just do not know what has gone on.'

    Mrs Campbell, who lives in Tuscon, Arizona, said her son had recently landed a new job.

    'I don't know what job it was but he was going to start very soon. He has held number of finance positions with companies.

    'We have been told by the police to wait for the results of the autopsy before the cause of death is known.'

    Asked if there were any problems in the family, she replied: 'No, no. They were fine. They had everything to live for. I just don't understand how this could happen.'

    Mrs Campbell said another of her sons was liaising with the Hillsborough County Sheriff's Office in Tampa.

    While police have yet to officially confirm the Campbell family to be the victims Mrs Campbell acknowledged her son and his family are dead.

    After learning about the early morning blaze she had tried in vain to contact her son on his phone but just received an engaged signal.

    Fire: The blaze was so ferocious on May 7 that firefighters could not get in to examine the grisly scene until the next day

    A relative later spoke with Sheriffs' deputies who told them the grim news.

    Mrs Campbell said her grandson Colin was looking forward to graduating from school next month.

    'He just loved baseball and wanted to be a baseball player,' she said.

    'He was such a lovely young man. Megan had just started at the school.'

    Mrs Campbell said the teen's mum Kimberly was a homemaker.

    'I have no clue what went on. I really am at a loss.'

    Kimberly Campbell's father, Gordon Lambie, described the family as close-knit and successful.

    Darrin Campbell had moved to Tampa more than 10 years ago to take a job with a glass container manufacturer, Lambie said. Recently, Mr Campbell had been volunteering at his children's private school, Carrollwood Day School, as the treasurer, an unpaid position.

    'I've lost my entire family,' Lambie said from his Michigan home. 'It's very tough right now because I'm 1,500 miles away.'

    The investigation into the deaths is still at an early stage but police have confirmed they are not looking for any other suspects.

    There were no 911 calls placed from within the home which is in the exclusive gated community of Avila Golf and Country Club north of downtown Tampa.

    Scorched: At least three people were discovered dead in the lavish home, pictured

     

    Meanwhile, it emerged Thursday Mr Campbell had bought fireworks days before the house was destroyed in the inferno, and a neighbor told a 911 dispatcher he heard firecrackers as the blaze burned.

    Mr Campbell purchased the fireworks on Sunday, William Weimer, vice president of Phantom fireworks, said Thursday by telephone from the company's Ohio headquarters.

    Mr Campbell bought six packages of small firecrackers and about that many packs of aerial fireworks designed to shoot into the air, said Weimer, who described them as backyard fireworks someone would shoot off on the Fourth of July.

    He said the fireworks could have started a fire, but it would have spread slowly. The amount of powder inside the fireworks was smaller than the size of an aspirin, he said.

    Neighbors described hearing the fireworks go off as the house burned.

    'Geez. What is that popping noise?' a male neighbor said on a 911 call.

    Another 911 caller, a security manager for the gated community, told dispatchers the fire was in the house's garage.

    Fire-fighters have described the scene inside the burnt-out mansion as 'unusual' with fireworks littered through the five-bedroom home rented from former tennis player Blake, who was ranked fourth in the world in 2006.

    Neighbors alerted the emergency authorities when they saw flames leaping from the home.

    Sad: Colin Campbell, 19, pictured left at prom and right on the baseball field, was an active sportsman while at school

    Tragic: Megan, 16, pictured left and right, attended Carrollwood day school, and the principal reportedly said this afternoon that both students will be missed

    It wasn't until at least six hours after the blaze was first reported that fire crews were able to go into the building where they made the grisly discovery.

    Friends and neighbors of the family are at a loss to understand what went on in the rented mansion.

    Mr Campbell, who has held a number of senior positions with companies in the Tampa region, last listed his workplace as IVANS, an insurance company.

    A spokesman for the Tampa based company where he was vice president said he left three years ago.

    Meanwhile, a spokesman for Vastec, Inc., a company specializing in digital storage, said Campbell had been their Chief Operating Officer.

    'On behalf of all in our organization we are greatly saddened to learn the news regarding our Chief Operating Officer, Darrin Campbell, and the Campbell family,' the representative said.

    'This is a difficult time for all involved and we are trying to cope with this news.We extend our heartfelt sympathies to friends and family members during this time of grief.'

    No other employment was listed on his LinkedIn profile.

    It is not known what job he was due to start or if he had been unemployed for a number of months.

    Both children attended Carrollwood day school where fees start at $15,000 a year.

    Staff were refusing to comment and security guards had been posted at the front gate.

    Devastation: Describing the scene inside the burnt-out mansion, pictured, as 'unusual,' firefighters said they discovered fireworks littered through the five-bedroom home

    Away: Blake, pictured with his wife, Emily, was reportedly at their Connecticut residence when the fire broke out

    According to her Twitter, Megan won an academic award at the school just a day before the deaths.

    Darrin and Kimberly met in Lansing, Michigan, when they both worked as legislative aides in the state legislature. Kimberly Campbell had graduated from Central Michigan University and Darrin Campbell had an MBA from the University of Michigan, Lambie said.

    They had lived in San Antonio, Texas, where Darrin Campbell was an executive with Pearl Brewing Company before they moved to the Tampa area around 2001 so he could take a job with Anchor Glass Container Corp.

    They sold their previous home but wanted to live in a place close to their children's school, so they signed a two-year lease for Blake's home, which was close enough for the teens to walk to school, Lambie said.

    Police said the family had been living there for about two years. Neither deputies nor the Department of Children and Families had been called to the home, officials said.

    A former neighbor, George Connley, said they were a nice family. He said that Kimberly Campbell was 'sophisticated and classy.'

    'We know nothing of any problems,' Connley said. 'The kids were outstanding children. This is very difficult to put our arms around.'

    Online, friends of the two teens expressed their grief in photos and tweets.

    Emergency response: It wasn't until at least six hours after the blaze was first reported that fire crews were able to go into the building where they made the grisly discovery of the bodies

    Under the #ripcampbell family one wrote: 'Heaven gained a few more angels today.'

    Others wrote about their shock at talking with Colin the day before he was killed.

    Maxx Black, from Lutz, Florida, wrote: 'I was really hoping I would wake up and it would have all been a dream.'

    Some students gathered Wednesday evening and released balloons with messages on them as a remembrance.

    'Our hearts go out to the family in connection with this tragedy. We have grief counselors on site and we are rallying around our students, faculty, and families,' the school said in a statement.

    The fire at the mansion was reported about 6 a.m. Wednesday.

    Blake bought the home in the Avila subdivision in 2005 for $1.5 million, according to Hillsborough County property records.

    Outpouring: Friends of the children, pictured left and right, expressed their grief online

    Award: Tragically, Megan had won an award at school just a day before her death

    Avila is an exclusive enclave known for its mansions and heavy security. Many well-known athletes call the community home and over the years, various football, baseball and tennis players have bought homes in the subdivision, which has a country club and golf course.

    The news comes after another harrowing 911 call was released by police, describing the moment James Blake's Florida mansion exploded into flames.

    A panicked neighbor described to emergency dispatchers how the two story Tampa mansion turned into a fireball before her eyes at around 5:40a.m.

    'Oh my god, I was was walking my dog and the house just exploded. It's just so horrible, I was walking by as it was exploding,' the woman says on the 911 call as she tries to keep calm. 'The house is engulfed in flames ma'am. It's a two story house. Oh my god.'

    When asked if there were people inside the home, she responds 'Yes ma'am, I'm sure. It was about 5:40 in the morning. There's a husband and a wife and two or three kids (who live there).'

    The 911 dispatcher initially thought the caller was reporting a 'fight' not a 'fire' and took almost two minutes to connect the call to the fire department. At that point, the neighbor had to explain again what was going on, and give the address of the inferno.
