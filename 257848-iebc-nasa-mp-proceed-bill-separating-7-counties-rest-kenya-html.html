    IEBC gives NASA MP go-ahead to proceed with his Bill for separating 7 counties from the rest of Keny

    - Homa Bay Town MP Peter Kaluma drafted a Bill seeking to have 40 counties separated from the rest of Kenya to form the People's Republic of Kenya - The IEBC gave him the green light to proceed with the Bill on Monday, November 13

    14 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - Homa Bay Town MP Peter Kaluma drafted a Bill seeking to have 40 counties separated from the rest of Kenya to form the People's Republic of Kenya

    - The IEBC gave him the green light to proceed with the Bill on Monday, November 13

    - Kaluma excluded 7 counties from Mount Kenya region- the backyard of President Uhuru Kenyatta

    The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission gave Homa Bay Town MP Peter Kaluma the green light to proceed with his secession Bill by either presenting it to Parliament of seek popular Kenyan vote in support of the initiative.

    Kaluma drafted a Bill seeking to have 40 counties seceded from the rest of Kenya to form the People’s Republic of Kenya.

    According to a report by the Daily Nation on Monday, November 13, IEBC Chairman Wafula Chebukati gave Kaluma the go ahead with his secession draft Bill.

    According to prior TUKO.co.ke reports, Kaluma was of the opinion that President Uhuru Kenyatta and his Jubilee regime had rigged elections, emasculated the Parliament, commissions and independent offices; and ran a systematic suppression of other groups and communities.

    Should he take the Parliamentary initiative, Kaluma would be required to introduce his Bill to either House in Parliament where Article 256(d) states that either House in the bicameral Parliament would have to give 2/3 of its assent to the Bill’s first and second readings for it to pass.

    The Bill would then be passed on to the President for assent and publication after requesting the IEBC to conduct a referendum within 90 days (Article 256 (5a). The bill would then be passed after 30 days if the IEBC approves that it was in accordance with Article 255 (2)

    Should Kaluma seek amendment by popular initiative, he would be required to have acquired one million registered voters’ signatures. He will then deliver the draft Bill and the signatures to the IEBC for verification.

    Following the verification, the IEBC will submit a draft of the Bill to the 47 county assemblies for consideration within 3 months after the date it was submitted to the IEBC.

    Article 256 (6) states that if the Assemblies approve the draft Bill, the speaker of each assembly would deliver a copy to the Speakers of the bicameral Parliament with a certificate that the County Assemblies approved it.

    If a majority of Assemblies approve it, it will be introduced in Parliament and will only be passed by a majority vote. If passed, it would then be submitted to the President for assent and publication after the IEBC conducts a referendum within 90 days (Article 256; 4-5).

    If either house fails to pass it, the Bill would then be submitted to Kenyans for a referendum.
