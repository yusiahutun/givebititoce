    The designer homeware edit | what to buy now

    These days, fashion needn't begin and end with your wardrobe. The chicest tastemakers are applying their sense of style not just to what they wear, but to how they live, with designer interiors becoming the new way to semaphore a carefully curated aesthetic.

    11 Mar 2024, 00:00

    These days, fashion needn't begin and end with your wardrobe. The chicest tastemakers are applying their sense of style not just to what they wear, but to how they live, with designer interiors becoming the new way to semaphore a carefully curated aesthetic.

    Globally, home design is a $643 billion market – one that understandably grew during the pandemic, when more time spent at home (and less out at restaurants, in the office and with friends) meant that we invested in our environments over our outfits. According to Euromonitor International, that market is set to grow at a 5 per cent annual growth rate through 2026 — much faster than it was growing in 2018 and 2019. So it's no surprise that luxury brands are getting in on the buzz.

    Now, the way to signal quiet luxury isn't just with a pair of boots from The Row, it's with Loro Piana's Andes-inspired furniture (Kendall Roy would be jealous). The glorious blanket slung over the sofa in your hostess' carefully designed apartment is now as likely to be from Hermès as the shoes in her wardrobe. And as for tableware? Emilia Wickstead, Aquazzura Casa and Gucci boast designs that you'll find present and correct at the most luxurious dinner parties.

    These are the best high-end brands selling homeware right now – and our pick of the most premium products to add to your own space.

    Dior

    Courtesy of Dior

    When it comes to A-list design houses that exemplify refined elegance, Dior is one name that that immediately springs to mind. "Living in a house which doesn't reflect who you are is like wearing someone else's clothes," Christian Dior once said, and now the brand's homeware collection is a fitting extension of its fashion line, showcasing graceful silhouettes and sophisticated prints.

    The latest 'Lotus' range, created by artistic director Cordelia de Castellane, celebrates the flower of the same name, which adorns earthenware crockery, a lacquered tray, table linens, hand-painted glassware, a carafe and elegant 'Salento' candleholders in delicate shades of blue and green. If you've long admired this classic French maison for its timeless appeal, consider its interiors collection for a touch of understated glamour at home.

    Emilia Wickstead

    Courtesy of Emilia Wickstead

    Loved by Gigi Hadid, Naomi Watts and Kate, the Princess of Wales, to name a few, Emilia Wickstead's contemporary updates on traditional designs have made her a household name. Happily for fans of the brand, this marriage of nostalgia with modern design extends to her homeware range, too. Focused on a core collection of simple yet elegant tabletop pieces, the selection includes china, linen and glassware in a palette of dark green, white and polished silver. The latest addition is a deep blue option.

    Taking inspiration from Wickstead's formative design years in Italy, the collection is just as beautifully made as her fashion line. Heavy crystal water and wine vessels are handblown in Tuscany by artisan glassmakers, while table linens featuring pretty, floral designs or embroidered geometric motifs are also produced in Tuscany by Angela Wickstead – Emilia's mother. The result is a collection made to exacting standards, designed to last a lifetime.

    Marni

    Courtesy of Marni x Serax

    The whimsical, oh-so-Italian designs of cult fashion house Marni have now been realised on a set of chic tableware, crafted in partnership with Belgian design label Serax and available exclusively in the UK at Selfridges. The 'Midnight Flowers' collection of porcelain and glassware is the luxury fashion brand’s first pivot into homeware, and encompasses plates, dishes, cups, wine and champagne glasses and teapots, all decorated with botanical-inspired prints intended to mix and match.

    Envisioned by creative director Francesco Risso, the sophisticated yet offbeat range is characteristic of Marni's quirky elegance with its gentle palette of mauve, teal and rose, the floral patterns reminiscent of those often seen in the fashion house's ready to wear collections. Highly covetable – especially as warmer weather approaches – this is the stylish answer to summer tablescaping.

    Loewe

    Courtesy of Loewe Home Scents

    That gorgeously scented candle burning in your most fashionable friend's living room? We'd be prepared to bet it's from Loewe Home Scents, the interiors fragrance outpost of the designer brand. These meticulously crafted candles hero scents that are far more complex than those offered by your average high-street brand: think tomato leaves, cucumber and a particularly subtle oregano fragrance, all intended to evoke the smell of fresh flowers and foliage.

    Pick up your favourite housed in a colourful, terracotta earthenware pot (that's ideal for storing make-up brushes and knick-knacks when the candle is burnt through). We, however, love the wax candleholders – their trompe-l'œil effect references the house's similarly cheeky fashion and accessories, and the generously scented wax will infuse any room with delicious fragrance, even when unlit.

    Sophie Bille Brahe

    Courtesy of Sophie Bille Brahe

    Fine-jewellery designer Sophie Bille Brahe counts Alicia Vikander, Rihanna, Emma Watson and Lily James among her fanbase, so the news that she would be launching an exclusive, limited-edition collection of Murano glass vases was met with much excitement by the style set.

    The simple, elegant pieces in the collection are inspired by Bille Brahe's signature 'Escargot' shape, reimagined for homeware; the 'Cellophane Escargot' vase incorporates the same spiral pattern into its design. We're also particular fans of the circular vases in blush-coloured and pearly-white hues, which reference Bille Brahe's strong ties to her iconic 'Peggy' necklace. All 10 of the vase designs are handmade in Italy and are, should you be lucky enough to get your hands on one, the most fashionable way to display your blooms.

    Celia B

    BERNARDO BARAGANO

    Cult Spanish brand Celia B (named for its founder, Celia Bernardo) is all about print, a mission statement that extends from its scalloped, feminine dresses to its bright and beautiful homeware. These designs are not for minimalists: the collection, which spans ceramics, table linen and bedding, is bold and punchy; but if you're looking to add a dash of vibrancy to your home, this will fit the bill.

    Choose from cotton tablecloths and napkins (all edged in Celia B's signature gold, scalloped embroidery); wavy placemats with joyful prints; fine bone china, gilt-accented plates; and frilled bedding that's far more enticing than your average set. Just like Celia B's main fashion line, the whole homeware range is very competitively priced for its superior quality, meaning you'll own – and love – your pieces for years to come.

    Completedworks

    Courtesy of Completedworks

    The cool girl's jewellery brand of choice, Completedworks, is known for its dramatic, off-beat shapes – you're bound to find a pair of the label's beaten gold, mismatched earrings in any stylish woman's arsenal. Now, Completedworks has created a capsule tableware collection, in collaboration with Net-A-Porter, that follows the same sculptural approach to design. "There’s something very satisfying about taking a traditional medium such as glassware and subverting it to a place of newness. Traditional designs are often associated with permanence and solidity, whereas what we have designed are pieces that have this visual element of being in transition," says Anna Jewsbury, Completedworks' creative director.

    The collection, which consists of 24 individual pieces, is all hand-blown from 100 per cent recycled glass, melted down from discarded broken bottles and windows. We love the wavy-edged, melted-effect plates, but our favourite piece is the playful, looped candlestick in green – buy several and mix and match for a unique tablescape.

    Tiffany

    Courtesy of Tiffany

    Those little blue boxes are the stuff jewellery dreams are made of. Now, interiors aficionados can have their own, not-quite-so-little blue box moment courtesy of the brand's homeware collections, which embody the delicate and considered ethos of the label.

    Our favourite range, designed by artistic director Lauren Santo Domingo, is inspired by family crests and serves as an expression of the brand's legacy and heritage. "Designing a coat of arms, so to speak, for Tiffany – honouring its own unique history – was a special endeavour," said Santo Domingo. "Incorporating the maple leaf, silversmithing tools, gemstones, the year of founding and the motto 'Return to Tiffany' in Latin naturally felt well-deserved." Including dinnerware, glassware, linen and more, it's a collection that exudes casual-yet-elegant grandeur.

    Versace

    Courtesy of Versace Home at Harrods

    Versace's clothing collections are the last word in bold glamour, whether that translates to gold-clad supermodels strutting down the runway, or Donatella herself posing in a tighter-than-tight dress, teamed with her signature smokey eye. The brand's homeware offering is no different: plenty of fringing, Medusa motifs and lots of gold is the order of the day here. Prices are (naturally) high for the epic pieces of furniture, but touches of opulence on a more modest budget come in the form of molten silver-and-gold vases, intricately painted china, fluffy throws and sumptuous, printed cushions.

    The brand has an outpost at Harrods, so you can experience the flamboyance first-hand: think the clash of Classical influences from Baroque and ancient Green culture with a fearless use of colour and thoroughly modern shapes.

    Hermès

    COURTESY OF HERMÈS

    Fans of understated, luxuriously low-key French style should look no further than Hermès for an interiors refresh. Simple wooden furniture, intricately woven textiles and offbeat objets d'art define the homeware offering from this Parisian stalwart – as well as, naturally, sumptuous leathers (what else would you expect from the home of the iconic Birkin?)

    The prices are high for big-ticket pieces, but there are plenty of trinkets – including jewellery trays, paperweights and photograph frames – that sit at the more affordable end of the spectrum. If you're willing to make the investment, however, Hermès homewares are just as well-made and tasteful as the label's fashion and accessories. Much like a classic bag, they're utterly timeless.

    Aquazzura Casa

    Courtesy of Aquazzura Casa / Alioth

    If any brand symbolises the more flamboyant side of la dolce vita, it's Aquazzura: known for its sky-high heels and diamanté, well, everything, this Italian brand has graced the feet of celebrities including Jennifer Lawrence and Nicole Kidman. Aquazzura may be a baby in the Italian style stakes (it celebrated its 10th anniversary last year) but it is expanding rapidly; most recently, the brand launched handbags and a homeware collection, Aquazzura Casa.

    In homage to founder Edgardo Osorio's love of hosting (unsurprisingly, his favourite cocktail is a party-ready spicy margarita), Aquazzura Casa's focus is on magnificent tableware, imbued with the brand's inimitable sense of theatrical style. Every piece is designed with thought and care down to the last millimetre, from cute raffia placemats to kitsch, pink-tinted champagne glasses. Maximalists, form an orderly queue.

    Henry Holland Studio

    courtesy of Henry Holland Studio

    A favourite on the Noughties LFW scene, Henry Holland is a name synonymous with poppy, irreverent style. His much-coveted slogan tee shirts (modelled by his fashion set friends, including Agyness Deyn, Daisy Lowe and Behati Prinsloo) catapulted him to fame, and became the building blocks of his punky, youthful label, House of Holland.

    These days, Holland has shelved fashion in favour of homeware, particularly ceramics, which he makes under his brand, Henry Holland Studio. Inspired by the 'Nerikomi' technique (which involves stacking coloured, earthenware clay before slicing through it to reveal distinctive patterns), all Holland's pottery pieces are made in collaboration with his Hackney-based team. The studio has expanded to make glassware and lighting, too, all with Holland's distinctive and quirky design stamp.

    Dolce & Gabbana

    Courtesy of Smeg x Dolce & Gabbana

    Fashion and cooking have long been apt bedfellows – after all, both pursuits hinge on good taste. While there are plenty of fashion-focused eateries (the Ralph Lauren restaurant in Paris and the Prada café in London to name a few), the really graceful gourmand will have their own kitchen outfitted by a designer label. Enter Smeg's popular collaboration with Italian megabrand, Dolce & Gabbana: a meeting of high-tech product and show-stopping design.

    While the oven and fridge would be a fabulous investment for the most dedicated cook, they are – like all D&G wares – priced to reflect their design heritage. More within reach of regular budgets, however, are smaller accessories such as blenders, juicers, kettles and toasters. For adding a touch of Italian flare to your breakfasts, you can't do better.
