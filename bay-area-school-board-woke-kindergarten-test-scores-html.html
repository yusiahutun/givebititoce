    A California school board member is facing a formal investigation after telling a colleague that she deserved to be 'strung up' for investing in a controversial 'Woke Kindergarten' program.

    Hayward School Board member Joe Ramos was questioning the district’s $250,000 splurge on an 'abolitionist' teaching program called Woke Kindergarten on January 24, despite appalling math and English scores. 

    He made the comments amid a crossfire exchange with Sandra Escobedo, director of supplemental and concentration services, as she fielded questions about the district’s budget.

    'Some of what you said today is alarming to me,' Ramos said. 'Some of these parents here, they should take a rope and string you up. I think there's something with you -' and was promptly cut off.

    Exclamations sounded from stunned parents in the audience, prompting board president Peter Bufete to call a point of order.

    Hayward School Board member Joe Ramos told colleague Sandra Escobedo: 'Some of these parents here, they should take a rope and string you up'

    Ramos later apologized for the remarks, but said the district offended him by pouring $250,000 into the Woke Kindergarten program

    The program aims to support 'children, families, educators and organizations in their commitment to abolitionist early education and pro-black and queer and trans liberation'

    'We are not gonna say things like that in this board meeting,' Bufete said.

    Ramos apologized before continuing his questioning of the program, which he said was run by 'socialists.'

    Woke Kindergarten bills itself as a 'global, abolitionist early childhood ecosystem and visionary creative portal' grounded in the idea that the education system as it is, steeped in racism and other oppressive structures, cannot be reformed.

    The program aims to support 'children, families, educators and organizations in their commitment to abolitionist early education and pro-black and queer and trans liberation,' according to its website.

    Founder Akiea 'Ki' Gross is black and nonbinary, going by they/them pronouns.

    The Hayward Unified School District has blown $250,000 worth of federal funding on a three-year contract with the program.

    However, the decision has been mired in controversy ever since test scores were reported to have dropped at the end of the second year of the contract.

    Glassbrook Elementary saw numeracy fall to four percent and literacy to 12 percent last spring. The scores represent a 4 percent decline in each category.

    At Glassbrook Elementary, numeracy fell to four percent and literacy to 12 percent at the conclusion of the district's second year with the Woke Kindergarten program

    Woke Kindergarten bills itself as a 'global, abolitionist early childhood ecosystem and visionary creative portal' founded by Akiea 'Ki' Gross, who uses they/them pronouns

    The curriculum includes a 'woke word of the day' including 'strike,' 'ceasefire' and 'protest'

    The elementary school also remains on the state's lowest-performing level on the Comprehensive School Improvement list and has a lower rate of attendance than before the program was introduced. 

    Woke Kindergarten has not been blamed for the slump in scores, but critics say the cash blown on the anti-racism program could have been far better spent on teaching students how to read and add up.  

    The district itself has an enrollment of roughly 18,000 students, two-thirds of whom are Hispanic or Latino and nearly 75 percent of whom hail from low-income families.

    Ramos apologized for the remarks on Monday, but said the district offended him by pouring $250,000 into the Woke Kindergarten program.

    A letter signed by several prominent administrators claimed Ramos had 'espoused hateful views' in the past as well, 'which members and allies have repeatedly denounced as incitements towards violence which put our students at risk.'

    'At last Wednesday’s board meeting, Trustee Ramos crossed the line and directly incited parents to execute an HUSD employee with whom he disagreed,' the letter continued. 

    In May, Ramos again drew the ire of the community for his statements opposing LGBTQ support in schools. 

    During a board meeting, he claimed the district was trying to 'indoctrinate' students through its pro-LGBTQ policies.

    'If you're homosexual, that's your business,' he said at the time. 'Don't bring it to the school house.'

    Ramos previously came under fire for statements opposing LGBTQ support in schools

    Ramos' colleagues slammed him for using 'language that evokes racial bias' towards Escobedo, a Hispanic woman

    Following his most recent remarks, the school board held a special closed session. Ramos was barred from participating.

    Heather Figueroa, a staff member who works with Escobedo on the education services team, described his comments as 'unconscionable.'

    'He clearly does not hold the same values as the rest of the district,' Figueroa said amid her defense of the Woke Kindergarten program.

    'He should be removed from his seat so he is not able to cause more damage to staff, students and families.'

    The four board members serving with Ramos later issued a statement saying the 'offensive and incendiary comment' was under formal investigation.

    The board cannot remove him from office. That can only be done through a recall or a regular election.

    'We cannot allow anyone at our public Board meetings to be demeaned or attacked in this manner,' Bufete, Sara Prada, April Oquenda and Ken Rawdon wrote in the statement.

    They noted that Ramos had used 'language that evokes racial violence.' Both he and Escobedo are Hispanic.

    The Woke Kindergarten curriculum refers to students as 'comrades' and claims to provide them with the 'language of the resistance'

    Hayward Superintendent Jason Reimann defended the district's choice to enroll in the program - though he admitted: 'I get that it's more money than we would have liked to have spent'

    The Woke Kindergarten program has faced criticism from educators themselves.

    Tiger Craven-Neeley, a third-grade teacher at Glassbrook Elementary, expressed that he found the training confusing.

    He said he was told a primary objective was to 'disrupt whiteness' in schools and that the sessions were 'not a place to express white guilt.'

    Craven-Neely found himself questioning the phrasing 'so-called United States' and lessons asking students to consider a world without police, money or landlords.

    The Woke Kindergarten curriculum includes a 'woke word of the day' including 'strike,' 'ceasefire' and 'protest.'

    It refers to students as 'comrades' and claims to offer them a 'language of the resistance...to introduce children to liberatory vocabulary in a way that they can easily digest, understand and most importantly, use in their critiques of the system.'

    Last week, Hayward Superintendent Jason Reimann told the Chronicle defended the district's choice to enroll in the program.

    'We are in favor 100% of abolishing systems of oppression where they hold our students back,' he said.

    'What I do believe is we should pick providers based on their work and how effective they are.'

    Reimann said Woke Kindergarten was not hired to improve test scores, but that 'helping students feel safe and whole is part and parcel of academic achievement.'

    However, he conceded: 'I get that it's more money than we would have liked to have spent.'
